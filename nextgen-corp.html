HR Analytics Case Study

HR Analytics & Workforce Insights

Client: NextGen Corp  |  Industry: Technology (Software & Hardware)  |  Tools: PostgreSQL, SQL

A SQL-driven HR analytics project designed to uncover insights into employee retention, performance distribution, attendance behaviour, and compensation fairness to support workforce planning.

Business Context

NextGen Corp is a rapidly scaling technology company operating across software and hardware divisions. As the workforce expanded, HR leadership faced challenges maintaining employee retention, consistent performance, and fair compensation practices across departments.

A centralized, data-driven HR analytics solution was required to identify workforce risks, performance gaps, and compensation inconsistencies.

Project Objectives

  • Analyse employee retention and turnover trends
  • Evaluate performance consistency across departments
  • Assess salary distribution and fairness relative to performance
  • Provide actionable insights to support HR strategy and workforce planning

Data Overview

Analysis was conducted using a relational database restored into PostgreSQL, consisting of the following tables:

  • Employees: profile, hire date, department, salary, performance score, attendance rate
  • Departments: department structure and classifications
  • Performance: historical performance scores for trend analysis
  • Attendance: presence and absence records
  • Turnover: employee exits and reasons for leaving
  • Salaries: salary history and adjustments over time

Analytical Approach

  • Restored and structured the HR dataset within PostgreSQL
  • Performed relational joins across employee, department, performance, and salary tables
  • Built SQL queries to calculate tenure, turnover rates, performance segmentation, and salary benchmarks
  • Segmented insights by department to identify high-risk and high-performing teams

Key Analysis Areas

Employee Retention & Turnover

  • Calculated turnover rates by department
  • Identified top long-tenured employees
  • Flagged employees at retention risk using performance and attendance indicators
  • Analysed primary reasons for employee exits

Performance Analysis

  • Segmented employees into high performers (5.0) and low performers (<3.5)
  • Computed average performance score by department
  • Identified departments with performance variability requiring intervention

Compensation & Salary Analysis

  • Calculated total salary expenditure
  • Computed average salary by job role
  • Identified high-earning employees (>$80,000)
  • Assessed salary alignment with performance across departments

Key KPIs Tracked

  • Turnover Rate by Department
  • Average Employee Tenure
  • Performance Score Distribution
  • Average Salary by Role and Department

Business Impact

  • Identified departments with elevated retention risk
  • Highlighted performance gaps requiring coaching or process improvements
  • Detected salary inconsistencies impacting fairness and satisfaction
  • Enabled data-driven HR decisions around retention and compensation strategy

Skills Demonstrated

PostgreSQL • SQL • Relational Joins • HR Analytics • Turnover Metrics • Performance Segmentation • Compensation Analysis • Insight Reporting
