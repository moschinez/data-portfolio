HR Analytics Case Study

HR Analytics & Workforce Insights

Client: NextGen Corp  |  Industry: Technology (Software & Hardware)  |  Tools: PostgreSQL, SQL

Introduction

Today, I’ll be walking you through an HR analytics case study built for NextGen Corp, a fast-growing technology company focused on innovation across software and hardware. The goal of this analysis is to provide a data-driven view of employee retention, performance trends, attendance patterns, and compensation fairness to support workforce planning and long-term growth.

Business Problem

As the company scaled, HR leadership identified rising concerns around:

  • Increasing employee turnover and retention risk
  • Performance variability across departments
  • Salary disparities impacting fairness and satisfaction

The HR team required a structured analytics approach to track trends, identify root causes, and support consistent, fair workforce decisions.

Project Objectives

  • Identify trends and patterns in employee retention and turnover
  • Evaluate performance across departments and over time
  • Assess the relationship between salary and performance to support fairness
  • Deliver actionable insights to guide HR strategy and workforce planning

Data Overview

Analysis was performed using a relational database restored into PostgreSQL, including the following tables:

  • Employees: employee profile, hire date, salary, department, performance score, attendance rate
  • Departments: department list and structure (Engineering, Sales, HR, Marketing, etc.)
  • Performance: monthly performance scores for trend analysis
  • Attendance: present/absent records to evaluate engagement and consistency
  • Turnover: employee exits and reasons for leaving
  • Salaries: salary history and changes over time

Approach & Methodology

  • Created the NextGen database and restored the provided dataset into PostgreSQL
  • Joined employee, department, performance, attendance, and salary tables using keys
  • Built SQL queries to compute turnover rate, tenure, performance distribution, and salary benchmarks
  • Segmented results by department to identify hotspots and improvement opportunities

Walkthrough: Key Analysis Areas

1) Employee Retention Analysis

This section focuses on turnover patterns and retention risk across departments. Key outputs included:

  • Identifying the top 5 longest-serving employees (tenure leaders)
  • Calculating turnover rate by department to highlight high-risk teams
  • Flagging employees at risk of leaving using performance and attendance indicators
  • Summarising the main reasons for employee exits to support corrective action

2) Performance Analysis

Performance analysis evaluated employee performance distribution and department consistency.

  • Measured the total number of employees who left the company
  • Segmented employees into high performers (5.0) and low performers (<3.5)
  • Identified departments with the highest concentration of high and low performers
  • Computed average performance score by department to benchmark teams

3) Salary Analysis

Salary analysis assessed compensation structure and fairness relative to performance.

  • Computed total company salary expense
  • Calculated average salary by job title
  • Identified employees earning above $80,000
  • Evaluated salary vs performance correlation across departments to detect gaps

Business Impact

  • Identified departments with elevated turnover risk requiring targeted interventions
  • Highlighted performance variability and areas needing coaching or process improvements
  • Detected salary inconsistencies impacting fairness and employee satisfaction
  • Supported data-driven HR decisions around retention planning and compensation alignment
